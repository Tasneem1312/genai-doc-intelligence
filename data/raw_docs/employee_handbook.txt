Employee Handbook — Leave Policy

Section 1: Annual Leave
All full-time employees are entitled to 18 days of paid annual leave per calendar year. Leave accrues monthly at a rate of 1.5 days per month. Unused annual leave can be carried forward to the next calendar year, up to a maximum cap of 10 days. Any leave beyond this cap is forfeited unless approved otherwise by HR in writing.

Section 2: Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to 12 days of paid sick leave per year. A medical certificate is required for sick leave exceeding 3 consecutive days. Sick leave does not carry forward to the next year and resets every January 1st.

Section 3: Maternity and Paternity Leave
Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave as per company policy, in line with the Maternity Benefit Act. Male employees are entitled to 2 weeks of paid paternity leave, which must be availed within 6 months of the child's birth.

Section 4: Work From Home Policy
Employees may request to work from home up to 2 days per week, subject to manager approval. Requests must be submitted at least 24 hours in advance through the HR portal. Employees in customer-facing roles may have restricted WFH eligibility based on team requirements.

Section 5: Notice Period
The standard notice period for resignation is 60 days for employees at the Associate level and above, and 30 days for employees below Associate level. The company reserves the right to waive part of the notice period at its discretion, or to require payment in lieu of notice.

Section 6: Reimbursement Policy
Employees can claim reimbursement for approved business expenses including travel, client meals, and work-related software subscriptions. All claims must be submitted within 30 days of the expense being incurred, accompanied by valid receipts. Claims submitted after 30 days require additional manager and finance approval.

Section 7: Performance Review Cycle
Performance reviews are conducted twice a year, in April and October. Employees are evaluated against goals set at the start of each cycle, and ratings directly influence annual compensation revisions and promotion eligibility.
